Frequently Asked Questions

What is PM2.5 and why is it dangerous?

PM2.5 refers to fine particles less than 2.5 micrometers in diameter. Because they are so small, they can bypass your respiratory system's natural filters, entering your lungs and even your bloodstream, potentially causing heart and lung issues.

When should I limit my time outdoors?

If the AQI reaches the "Unhealthy" range (over 150), everyone should limit prolonged outdoor exertion. However, sensitive groups—like children, the elderly, or those with asthma—should take precautions once levels cross 100.

Where does this data come from?

Our data is fetched in real-time from the nearest official government monitoring station via the WAQI API. If your specific city isn't listed, the app automatically finds the closest reporting station to give you the most accurate local estimate.
