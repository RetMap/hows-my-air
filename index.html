Frequently Asked Questions

What is PM2.5?

PM2.5 refers to fine particulate matter with a diameter of less than 2.5 micrometers. These particles are particularly dangerous because they can penetrate deep into the lungs and even enter the bloodstream.

When should I stay indoors?

If the Air Quality Index (AQI) exceeds 150, everyone should limit outdoor exposure. For sensitive groups, including children and those with asthma, precautions should start when levels cross 100.
